<p align="center"><font size="7" color="#FF0000">SOIL POLLUTION</font></p>
<p style="color: rgb(32, 33, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: start; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial; margin: 0.5em 0px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255)">
<b><font size="6">Soil Contamination</font></b><font size="6">,&nbsp;<b>Soil 
pollution</b>, or&nbsp;<b>Land Pollution</b>&nbsp;as a part of&nbsp;<a class="new" title="Land Degradation (page does not exist)" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(186, 0, 0); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Land_Degradation&action=edit&redlink=1">Land 
Degradation</a>&nbsp;is caused by the presence of&nbsp;<a title="Xenobiotic" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xenobiotic">Xenobiotic</a>&nbsp;(human-made) 
chemicals or other alteration in the natural soil environment. It is typically 
caused by industrial activity, agricultural chemicals or improper disposal of&nbsp;<a title="Waste" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waste">waste</a>. 
The most common chemicals involved are&nbsp;<a title="Petroleum" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petroleum">petroleum</a>&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Hydrocarbons" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrocarbons">hydrocarbons</a>,&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Polynuclear aromatic hydrocarbons" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynuclear_aromatic_hydrocarbons">polynuclear 
aromatic hydrocarbons</a>&nbsp;(such as naphthalene and&nbsp;<a title="Benzo(a)pyrene" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzo(a)pyrene">benzo(a)pyrene</a>),&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Solvents" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solvents">solvents</a>, 
pesticides,&nbsp;<a title="Lead" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lead">lead</a>, 
and other&nbsp;<a title="Heavy metals" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heavy_metals">heavy 
metals</a>. Contamination is correlated with the degree of&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Industrialization" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background: none" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrialization">industrialization</a>&nbsp;and 
intensity of chemical substance.</font></p>

<font size="6">Noise pollution is the constant presence of loud, disruptive 
noises in an area. Usually, noise pollution is caused by construction or nearby 
transportation facilities, such as airports.</font></span></p>

<span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: GeographEditWeb, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: left; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0.8px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial; display: inline !important; float: none; background-color: rgb(250, 250, 250)">
<font size="6">Noise pollution is unpleasant, and can be dangerous. Some 
songbirds, such as robins, are unable to communicate or find food in the 
presence of heavy noise pollution. The sound waves produced by some noise 
pollutants can disrupt the sonar used by marine animals to communicate or locate 
food.</font></span></p>
